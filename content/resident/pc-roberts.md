PC Roberts was a police constable who served in the Stanton-by-Dale area in the early 1920s.

In November 1922, he gave evidence at Ilkeston Petty Sessions in a case involving juvenile traders selling firewood at Stanton-by-Dale, contributing to the prosecution under bye-laws relating to the employment of children.
