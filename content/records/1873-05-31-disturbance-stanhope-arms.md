**PETTY SESSIONS, Thursday, May 20th.**  
[Before W. T. Cox, Esq., and Colonel Newdigate.]

**A PUBLIC-HOUSE DISTURBANCE.—**Edward Burrows, of Stanton-by-Dale, was charged with being disorderly on the premises of Richard Scattergood, of the Stanhope Arms, Stanton-by-Dale. The case was proved by Scattergood, who said he had kept the Stanhope Arms between 30 and 40 years without a charge being brought against him.—Fined 10s. and 17s. 6d. costs.
