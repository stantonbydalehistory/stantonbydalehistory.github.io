title: "Stanton Hall"
street: "Main Street"
institutions: []
---

A comfortable and genteel dwelling house advertised for let in 1842, containing dining, drawing, and breakfast rooms, with two kitchens and water closet on the ground floor.

The property included a coach-house for two carriages, stabling for five horses with lofts and dove-cote, greenhouse, extensive pleasure and kitchen gardens, and several closes of rich grassland totaling 24 acres, 3 roods, and 15 perches.

The rental also included shooting rights over the Manor of Stanton, containing 1400 acres.

In 1842, the occupier was Laurence Hall, Esq.
